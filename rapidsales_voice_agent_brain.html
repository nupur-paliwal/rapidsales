S-01

Opening Scripts — Two Versions

Designed to establish context in under 15 seconds, earn permission to continue, and never sound like an IVR. Spoken in natural, conversational Hindi-inflected English.

◎
Voice design principle: The opening must do three things before the homeowner's internal "is this a scam?" detector fires — which happens at ~6 seconds. (1) Sound like a specific person with a reason to call. (2) Reference something locally relevant. (3) Hand the conversation over with a short, easy question that doesn't feel like a pitch.

Homeowner Picks Up Directly

Version A
🎙 AI Agent · Arjun
"Hello, {First Name} ji? 0.4s Namaste — I'm Arjun calling from SunPower Solar. I'll take just a minute of your time.

We've been helping a few homeowners in {Neighbourhood / City} bring their electricity bills down by about 80 to 90 percent — and honestly, most of them were surprised how straightforward the whole thing was.

I just wanted to check — is your electricity bill something that's been on your mind lately?"
Duration
~11 sec
Design notes: "Ji" signals respect — disarms the cold-call reflex. Localising to their neighbourhood creates relevance without being creepy. "Is your bill on your mind" is a low-stakes yes/no that almost everyone says yes to — it's a micro-commitment, not a pitch.

Family Member Answers

Version B
🎙 AI Agent · Arjun
"Hello, good {morning/afternoon/evening}! 0.3s I'm Arjun from SunPower Solar.

I was actually hoping to reach the owner of the house — someone who handles the electricity and home matters. 0.4s Is that person available for just a minute?

If not, no problem at all — I can try back at a better time. What would work?"
Duration
~9 sec
Design notes: "Owner of the house" is better than "husband" or "head of household" — avoids gender assumptions common in Indian homes. The "no problem at all — what would work?" turn is crucial: it treats the family member as an ally, not a gatekeeper, and often produces a callback slot.
◎
What happens next: If the homeowner says "yes, my bill is high" → the agent moves to a 2-question discovery. If they say "not really" → the agent pivots to a subsidy/incentive hook: "Understood — actually the reason I'm calling is there's a central government subsidy running right now that a lot of homeowners don't know about..."
S-02

Objection Handling — 5 Complete Branches

Each branch includes: the AI's exact spoken response, the strategic reasoning, and the follow-up move if the prospect softens.

🚫
Not Interested
"Nahi chahiye, thank you." / "Not interested."
Medium
AI Response — Exact Script
"Absolutely, {Name} ji, I completely understand. 0.5s I'm not trying to sell you anything right now — I just wanted to share something that's saving homeowners in your area around ₹2,000 to ₹3,000 every month on their electricity bill.

Can I leave you with just one number? The government subsidy is covering up to 40% of the installation cost right now — and most people didn't know it existed. Even if it's not for you today, it might be useful someday."
Strategy: Validate the objection → depressurize by removing the "sales" frame → plant a specific, credible data point (₹2–3K/month saving). The PM Surya Ghar subsidy reference is real and authoritative — it reframes the call as informational, not transactional.
Follow-up Move (if prospect softens / asks "what subsidy?")
"The government's PM Surya Ghar scheme covers 40% of the installation — so on a ₹3.5 lakh system, you'd only pay around ₹2 lakh after subsidy. And that cost comes back in electricity savings in about 4 to 5 years — after that it's pure savings for 20-plus years.

I can arrange a free site visit just to check if your rooftop qualifies — zero obligation. Would that be okay?"
☀️
Already Have Solar
"Hamne pehle hi solar lagwaya hai." / "We already have solar."
Soft
AI Response — Exact Script
"Oh wonderful! That's actually great to hear. 0.4s How long has the system been running — is it 2 years or older?

I ask because the newer systems have significantly better efficiency and the subsidy structure is different now — many homeowners who installed 3+ years ago are actually upgrading or adding capacity because their power needs have gone up."
Strategy: Compliment the decision (instant rapport). Then use a discovery question about age — this naturally opens a crack. Older systems are a genuine upgrade opportunity; newer ones might be undersized. This response turns a hard stop into a genuine conversation.
Follow-up Move (if they say "it's 3 years old" or "output has gone down")
"Yes, that's exactly what's happening — after 3 to 5 years, panel efficiency typically drops by 10 to 15%. And if your household electricity use has gone up, the original system may no longer be enough.

Our team does a free rooftop audit — they can tell you in 30 minutes whether your current system is performing optimally or whether there's a gap. Want me to schedule that?"
💸
It's Too Expensive
"Bahut mehnga hai." / "3 lakh is too much."
Medium
AI Response — Exact Script
"You know, {Name} ji, that's the most common thing people say before they see the actual breakdown. 0.5s

After the government subsidy, a standard 3kW system comes down to roughly ₹1.5 to 1.8 lakh. And with zero-cost EMI on most bank cards — that's about ₹5,000 to 7,000 a month. Meanwhile, your current electricity bill is probably already in that range, right?

So essentially you're replacing a bill with an asset — one that generates free power for 25 years."
Strategy: "That's the most common thing people say before..." is a social-proof reframe that doesn't dismiss the concern. Then anchor on the post-subsidy number (not the sticker price). The "replacing a bill with an asset" line is the psychological pivot — it reframes cost as a substitution, not an addition.
Follow-up Move (if they say "tell me more about EMI")
"Most leading banks — HDFC, SBI, ICICI — offer no-cost EMI for solar. Typically 12 to 36 months. On a net cost of ₹1.7 lakh after subsidy, you're looking at around ₹5,600 a month for 30 months — and your bill goes down by ₹2,000 to 3,500 simultaneously.

Our advisor can run the exact numbers for your house in about 10 minutes. Would a quick call with them tomorrow work?"
🕐
Call Me Later
"Abhi busy hoon." / "Call me after 7." / "Not a good time."
Soft (not a rejection)
AI Response — Exact Script
"Of course, {Name} ji — I completely understand. 0.4s I don't want to take your time when you're busy.

Just so I can have everything ready when we speak — would tomorrow evening around 6 or 7 PM work for you? Or is there a time that's usually better?"
Strategy: Never say "I'll call you sometime" — that's a dead end. Always offer two specific time slots. This forces a micro-decision (6 or 7?) rather than a yes/no on the callback itself. Whoever picks the time is subconsciously more committed to the call.
Follow-up Move (if they give a specific time)
"Perfect — {Day}, {Time} it is. I'll make sure someone from our team calls you then. 0.3s And just to confirm — this is the right number to reach you on?

Thank you, {Name} ji. Have a great rest of your day!"
🔒
How Did You Get My Number?
"Aapko mera number kahan se mila?"
High — Trust Crisis
AI Response — Exact Script
"That's a completely fair question, {Name} ji — and I'm glad you asked. 0.5s

Your contact details are part of a registered homeowner database that complies with TRAI guidelines — similar to how banks or insurance companies reach out. We never share or sell numbers.

If you'd prefer not to receive any more calls from us, I can mark that right now — no questions asked. Or if you have even a small interest in solar, I'm happy to continue. What would you prefer?"
Strategy: This is a trust crisis — the only move is radical transparency and immediate control given back to the prospect. Mentioning TRAI legitimises the call. The "no questions asked DNC" offer paradoxically increases trust and often keeps the conversation alive — people who feel heard and in control are more open to continuing.
Follow-up Move (if they say "okay fine, what is this about?")
"Thank you for giving me a moment. 0.3s In short — we help homeowners in {City} cut their electricity bills by 80 to 90%, using rooftop solar with the government's 40% subsidy.

I just wanted to check if your power bill is something that adds up for you — is it currently around ₹2,000 or more a month?"
S-03

Intent Detection Layer — 6 Outcomes

Precise signal definitions with exact trigger phrases and automated system actions. These must be classifiable by the STT → NLU pipeline without ambiguity.

INTERESTED
INT_HOT
Exact signal phrases (any of these)
"haan, bill bahut aata hai" "kitna kharcha aayega" "subsidy milegi?" "how much does it cost" "tell me more" "site visit" [asks price / EMI] [asks about installation timeline]
Automated action
🔥Trigger Workflow D: discovery questions → warm handoff to human rep within 90 seconds. Flag as HOT_LEAD in CRM. Set follow-up SLA: 15 min.
CALLBACK REQUESTED
INT_CB
Exact signal phrases (any of these)
"baad mein call karo" "abhi busy hoon" "call me at [time]" "evening pe karo" "kal call karo" [gives specific time voluntarily] [line drops after initial positive signal]
Automated action
📅Extract time slot via NLP. Schedule callback in CRM. Send WhatsApp confirmation: "Arjun will call you on [Day] at [Time]." Tag: WARM_CB.
SOFT OBJECTION
INT_SOFT
Exact signal phrases (any of these)
"sochna padega" "wife se poochhna hai" "it's expensive" "not sure" "maybe" [hesitation + no clear rejection] [asks a question then goes quiet]
Automated action
🌡Deploy relevant objection-handling branch. After 2 attempts, offer callback. Tag: WARM_NURTURE. Enqueue for WhatsApp follow-up drip (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7).
HARD DNC
INT_DNC
Exact signal phrases (any of these — even once)
"DNC karo" "dobara mat call karna" "mujhe pareshan mat karo" "TRAI mein complain karunga" "remove my number" "stop calling" [abusive language directed at agent]
Automated action
🚫AI immediately says DNC acknowledgement. Hard-flag number in all systems. Zero future outreach on ANY channel. Log: DNC_EXPLICIT. No retry logic. Compliance audit trail created.
WRONG NUMBER
INT_WN
Exact signal phrases (any of these)
"wrong number" "yeh {Name} nahin hai" "this is a shop / office" "number badal gaya hai" "who?" [answers with business name] [confirms different name than lead record]
Automated action
📵Apologise gracefully. End call. Flag lead as WRONG_NUMBER in CRM. Suppress from all future campaigns. Trigger data quality alert to ops team.
BUSY / NO ANSWER
INT_NA
Exact signals (system-level detection)
Voicemail/IVR detected within 5s No pickup after 5 rings "abhi busy hoon, kuch mat bolo" (engaged tone) Call dropped within 3s of pickup [call duration < 8 seconds total]
Automated action
🔁No voicemail drop (bad practice). Schedule retry: Attempt 2 = +4 hours, Attempt 3 = next day (different time slot). After 3 no-answers: send SMS intro. After 5 = mark UNRESPONSIVE, move to low-priority pool.
◎
Edge case — ambiguous signals: "Sochta hoon" (I'll think about it) can be either SOFT_OBJECTION or a polite DNC depending on tone and context. The NLU layer must use both transcript text AND sentiment score from the voice signal (flat/agitated tone = DNC risk; normal/curious tone = SOFT). When confidence < 0.72 on the classification, default to CALLBACK REQUESTED — never risk a DNC violation by pushing forward on uncertain signals.
S-04

Warm Handoff Script — AI to Human Rep

The handoff must be invisible to the prospect — they should feel like they're still in the same conversation, not being transferred. The AI passes full structured context in the background before stepping back.

01
AI Confirms Interest & Sets Expectation AI Speaks
"That's great, {Name} ji! Based on what you've told me — your monthly bill is around ₹{Bill Amount} and your roof has good sunlight — this sounds like a very strong case for solar.

I want to make sure you speak with Rahul from our technical team — he's handled dozens of homes in {Neighbourhood} and can give you exact numbers for your house. 0.5s I'm going to bring him on the call right now — it'll just take a few seconds."
Why this works: Names the rep (humanises). References neighbourhood (credibility). Uses "your house" (personalisation). "Bring him on the call" is better than "transfer you" — it suggests a three-way conference, not a drop-off. The 0.5s pause creates space so the prospect doesn't feel rushed.
02
Background Context Packet Transmitted System · Silent
// Structured handoff packet — pushed to rep's screen/earpiece before they speak { "prospect": { "name": "{First Name} {Last Name}", "city": "{City}", "neighbourhood": "{Neighbourhood}" }, "call_summary": { "monthly_bill": "{₹X,XXX — self-reported}", "ownership": "Owner / Renter", "roof_type": "Flat / Sloped — confirmed", "objections_handled": ["cost", "subsidy_asked"], "sentiment": "Positive — asked about EMI", "hot_interest_signal": "Asked 'kitna lagega total?'" }, "suggested_next_step": "Offer free site visit + WhatsApp quote", "do_not_ask": ["name", "city", "bill amount — already captured"] }
do_not_ask is the most important field. Nothing kills a warm handoff faster than the rep asking "Can I get your name?" after the AI already collected it. The context packet prevents this and makes the rep sound like they were listening the whole time.
03
Rep Joins — AI Steps Back Human Rep Speaks
"Hello {Name} ji! Rahul here — Arjun just filled me in. So your bill is around ₹{Bill Amount} and you're in {Neighbourhood}? 0.4s Perfect — homes in that area generally get excellent solar output.

Let me just ask you one quick thing — is your rooftop accessible, or is it the kind you don't usually go up to?"
Rep briefing note: Open with a statement, not "How are you?" — it signals the rep already knows the context. The rooftop accessibility question is a forward-motion qualification that feels natural and moves the conversation toward the site visit close. Do not re-pitch solar — the prospect is already warm.
04
AI Exit (if prospect asks "who is this?") AI — Backup Only
"Yes — Rahul is our senior solar advisor in {City}. He'll be your single point of contact from here. I'll let him take it from here — good luck, {Name} ji!"
S-05

What This Flow Doesn't Handle — 3 Live Failures

Gaps that exist in the current design — identified from how real voice AI calls break in production, not from a theoretical checklist.

1

The Mid-Objection Interruption — "Baat karo, main sun raha hoon"

Prospect interrupts the AI mid-sentence and the STT pipeline doesn't catch the barge-in cleanly, causing the AI to keep speaking over them or respond to a partial transcript.

In a real voice call, Indian homeowners often talk over the AI — especially when they're curious ("acha, kitna kharcha?") or impatient ("theek hai, theek hai"). Current ASR pipelines have 200–400ms latency before they detect a barge-in. In that window, the AI may complete a full sentence the prospect didn't need to hear, which makes it sound robotic and not listening. The NLU then sees a partial transcript — "acha kitna" — and either mis-classifies it as a soft signal or loses it entirely. This doesn't just produce a bad interaction — it produces a bad signal that corrupts the intent classification.

Future Iteration Fix
Implement Voice Activity Detection (VAD) with 80ms sensitivity and configure the TTS engine to have "interruptible" utterances — the AI stops mid-sentence on barge-in rather than completing it. The STT pipeline must buffer the partial transcript and attempt a re-parse with the full interruption context before classifying. Additionally, train a lightweight barge-in classifier that distinguishes "agreeing interrupt" ("haan haan") vs "objection interrupt" ("ruko, ek minute") — these require different responses even if the words are similar.
2

The Three-Party Household — "Abhi mera beta ghar pe nahin hai"

The person who answers and engages positively is not the decision-maker. The actual homeowner is unavailable, out, or the spouse is involved — and the AI has no protocol for this.

Indian household decisions — especially ₹3–5 lakh purchases — rarely happen without spousal or family consensus. The current flow handles Version B (family member answers) at the start, but has no branch for when this is revealed mid-conversation: "Sab sunta hoon, lekin signing toh ghar wale milke karenge." The AI has now built rapport with the wrong person. If it pushes for a handoff to a rep, it's pushing someone who can't close. If it books a callback, it doesn't know who to ask for. This produces callbacks that go nowhere.

Future Iteration Fix
Add a "decision-maker mapping" branch triggered when the AI detects a third-party signal mid-call: "main akela decide nahin kar sakta", "wife ko poochhna", "ghar mein sabko batana". The AI should acknowledge this positively ("That makes complete sense — solar is a household decision") and then shift the objective: instead of booking a rep call, offer to send a WhatsApp summary that both can review together, and ask: "When would be a good time for all of you to have a 15-minute call together?" This converts the call from a solo pitch to a household consultation appointment — much higher close rate.
3

The Rented Rooftop Problem — "Ghar mera nahin hai, main kiraye pe rehta hoon"

The prospect reveals mid-call that they're a tenant, not a homeowner. The entire pitch — subsidies, ownership returns, 25-year asset — is irrelevant to them, but the AI has no exit that recovers value from the call.

Rented vs. owned is not captured reliably in most B2C lead databases. A significant portion of Tier 2 city leads are renters who live in good-quality housing and have high electricity bills — they pass the surface-level filter but are disqualified by ownership. The current flow has no branch for this scenario, which means the AI either continues a now-irrelevant pitch (wasted call time) or abruptly ends the call (missed referral or future opportunity). This is both an efficiency problem and a data quality problem — the number is being called again in the next campaign.

Future Iteration Fix
Add a rooftop ownership qualifier as the second question after the opening (currently missing entirely). Phrasing: "Do you own the home or is it a rental?" If rented: pivot immediately — "Got it. We typically work with homeowners — but if you ever know someone who owns their place and has a high bill, they'd benefit a lot from this. Is that okay?" This converts a dead lead into a referral opportunity and logs the number as TENANT_DISQUALIFIED to prevent future calls. The CRM must also use this signal to retroactively clean similar leads in the same database batch.
